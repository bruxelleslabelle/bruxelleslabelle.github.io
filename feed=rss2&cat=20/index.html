It belongs to Pika Pika Japan, a new shop selling toys, items and clothes all the way from Japan. 

A while ago, there was a similar shop with the same name (and by the same owner) down Rue Lombard in the city centre. I never walked in the shop, but the day I was planning to do so, it was shut. When walking on Chaussée de Waterloo one day, (on my way to Bang Bang, to be precise), I noticed this new shop and walked in right away.

Lots of colours, lots of shapes, lots of cute little toys staring at you with big eyes. Inflatable animals, notebooks, mobile phone pendants, socks, piggy banks, stickers, markers … an incredible selection of Japanese stuff. If you’re not sure what you’re looking at, the owner and the shop assistant have tons of stories to tell.

I loved the little green and orange bird that tweeted every now and again, when  somebody walks passed it. But eventually left the shop with a bright red Kewpie, a plastic adorable little figurine, with wings and big eyes. It doesn’t do anything, but when I look at it, I cannot but smile.

Note: Everything is directly imported from Japan, which explains the price. Nothing it too expensive, though.

525 a, Chaussée de Waterloo, open every day but Sunday, between 11.00-19.00

This one on Avenue Louise just opened (hence the waving bird at the door), and sells ‘educational’ toys for kids from 0 to 12 years of age.

Eurekakids is actually a chain store, by there’s only one of them in Brussels. The shop is to be found near Rue du Bailli, you can’t miss it as it’s very colourful.

Once inside, you’ll find jigsaws, puppets, drawing kits, ‘make your own soap’ sets and more. Everything looks of great quality, and judging by the screaming kids, they loved it too.

I was a bit baffled by the large choice of kid-sized cleaning tools. Brushes, mops, even a whole cleaning lady trolley. It seems a bit wrong to give a brush and a dustpan as a gift for a toddler’s birthday, but they -apparently- love it.

The shop opened just in time for Saint Nicholas and Santa Claus, perfect if you haven’t got a clue what to give the kids this year.

199a, Avenue Louise, open from Tuesday until Saturday, between 10.30-18.30

]]> http://www.bruxelleslabelle.com/feed=rss2&p=1397 1 Les Petits Poissons http://www.bruxelleslabelle.com/p=1330 http://www.bruxelleslabelle.com/p=1330#comments Sun, 21 Oct 2012 16:00:55 +0000 http://www.bruxelleslabelle.com/p=1330 Now this is a great shop for kids (and their parents, and everybody who loves pretty things that make you laugh). Furniture, wooden toys, lamps, mobiles, funny cards, cute clothes, artwork… all under one roof.

The shop got its name from a French nursery rhyme. Little fish can swim as fast as the big ones do. A truth the owner takes very seriously, as she decided to open a little shop with a different approach than the big toy shops around.

Owner Julie Gerbaud* is a trained furniture restorer. As the current crisis prevented her from using her skills every day, she decided to change course. With Les Petits Poissons (the little fish) she now has her own kids shop, a universe for children and their parents.

On second hand fairs and flea markets, she buys old wooden toys and furniture which she gives a second life in the workshop behind her shop. From artisans, she buys paper lamps, pretty mobiles and handmade puppets.  From a shop that’s gone bust, she bought a series of cute clothes for kids, and from a couple of befriended artists, she exposes and sells some drawing and paintings for kids…. Loved it!

* The name might ring a bell. Laurent Gerbaud is a famous chocolate maker, and Julie is his sister.

New address!: 68 Place du Jeu de Balle, open from Wednesday until Saturday between 12.00-18.00

THIS SHOP IS NOW CLOSED. YOU’LL FIND LA MEUTE ON THIS LOCATION.

And another kids store. Even though the shop has been open for a year now, I never really noticed it when walking down Rue du Page. Until that one day …

First of all, apparently I am not the only one who walks passed the shop. Even though it’s located in one of Ixelles’s most lively streets, it also is situated at the complete rear of the street (away from bustling Place du Chatelain).

The shop owner started years ago, with a blog for pregnant women. She soon opened a webshop with cool stuff for kids, and a few years later she wanted to meet her customers and opened a ‘real store’. She sells toys (made from natural fabrics such as linen) and a few exclusive clothes lines as well.

Everything she sells has been tried and tested by her two kids, that’s clever! Little Circus is a great place to find special and original gifts for kids!

92, Rue du Page, open from Wednesday until Saturday, from 11.00-18.00
