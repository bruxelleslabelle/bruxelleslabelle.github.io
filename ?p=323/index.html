Les Penates

Posted by Bruxelles la Belle on July 17, 2011 in Drink, Eat, Ixelles |
Subscribe

There’s nothing not to love about Les Penates: the food and drinks are tasty, the interior looks nice and the garden is a real bonus on sunny days.

This bar / eatery is located in an old butcher’s shop, and the new owners kept some of those features: the white tiles on the wall and the huge fridge at the front. You can both sit inside or outside on the small terrace facing the street or the larger one in the garden.

The menu reads a mix of easy dishes such as soups, quiches and special sandwiches. You can tell everything is fresh and homemade, as some dishes are no longer available if you come for a late lunch. (Luckily there always is some cake or pie left!)

Les Penates has a large selection of wines, all at a correct price. The service is quick and friendly. Oh, and the name ‘penates’ refers to Roman house deities … I’m sure they’d approve of this cozy place.

42, Rue des Vergnies, open every day from 11.00-00.00 (01.00 on Friday and Saturday)
