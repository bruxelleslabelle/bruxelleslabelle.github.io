La Grande Epicerie

Posted by Bruxelles la Belle on April 23, 2012 in Drink, Eat, Uccle |
Subscribe

This luxury supermarket is located in Fort-Jaco (Uccle), an area that doesn’t feel like Brussels at all. With plenty of upscale boutiques in the area, food shopping at La Grand Epicerie feels rather fancy too.

It doesn’t really look like much from the outside, but wait until you’re inside. First you’ll see the neatest arranged fruit and vegetables section, with purple Vitelotte potatoes and the shiniest apples. Then comes the drinks section, where I always stop and look for juices or lemonades I’ve never drunk before. This time I brought home some lemongrass infused water by Ganíc and some Italian chinotto lemonade for my brother.

I love their tea and coffee section, as there is so much to chose from. And of course, no tea time is complete without a nibble or two from the biscuit section. (which, as to be expected stocks some fancy biscuits and cakes ).

They have an English food section too, including the classic such as brown sauce, golden syrup, English mustard and cider vinegar. Same goes for some Mediterranean and kosher ingredients.

I was pleased to see their large range of baking supplies, including nearly all the Dr Oetker ingredients (I finally found the fruit tart topping they no longer sell in regular supermarkets).

The meat and cheese counter looked great too, but as I was in a bit of a hurry, I didn’t have the time to check it all out as well.

At the till, my goods were carefully packed by a student and I left the shop as a happy customer. Doing all of your grocery shopping over there would be an attack on your food budget, but I do recommend an occasional trip if you’re looking for something different. All in all, it reminded me somewhat of Rob’s, only a lot smaller.

1331, Chaussée de Waterloo, open from Monday until Saturday, between 08.30-19.00
