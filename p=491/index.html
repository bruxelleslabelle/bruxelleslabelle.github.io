De Markten

Posted by Bruxelles la Belle on September 3, 2011 in City Centre, Drink, Eat |
Subscribe

A drink or light bite to eat on a terrace in a car-free street. That sounds nice, right? De Markten is the place you’re looking for such an ‘experience’.

You’ll find the street with café De Markten halfway down shopping paradise Rue Dansaert. Waving trees, tables and seats, even some lounge chairs … and -the odd one out – an accordion player.

De Markten is a cultural centre and meeting place for the Dutch speaking community in Brussels, but don’t worry, you’ll have a good time no matter what language you speak.

On the menu: lots of refreshing drinks and bites to eat (sandwiches, toasties, lasagna, etc).  If you’re starving, be patient, as the waiters are always busy running around and it might take a while before they notice you’re waving for attention.

On rainy days, sitting inside the café is quite nice too. They have a large section of newspapers and magazines to read, and yummy cakes to make up for the bad weather.

5 Rue du Vieux Marché aux Grains, open every day between 08.30-24.00, Sundays between 10.00-18.0
