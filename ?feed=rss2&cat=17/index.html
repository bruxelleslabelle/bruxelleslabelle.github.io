No more trips to London or Paris, the very best clothing, cosmetics and interior deco brands are all to be found at the enormous concept store Smets.

Smets is the name of the family who founded the shop in Luxemburg, in 1986. The first Belgian shop is located near Place Meiser, and is about 3.000 square meters large. Enough space to find some of my favourite brands together: Marc Jacobs, Isabel Marant, Mcq by McQueen, Alexander Wang, … Shoe addicts will be pleased to see Manolo Blahnik among the many other brands.

If, like me, you change your nail varnish as often as you change your socks, you’ll be pleased to find the whole OPI collection, as well as a lovely lady who’ll happily do your nails for only a fiver! Other make-up brands include Bobbi Brown, Bumble and Bumble, Essie and Christophe Robin.

To spice up your interior, Smets also sells a large collection of furniture by Kartell for instance. Scented candles come from Belgian brand Baobab and I also saw some nice ones in a Keith Haring decorated cup. I was quite pleased to discover a whole collection of tea by the Parisian brand Mariages Frères too.

If you’re getting a bit hungry while shopping, or need a glass of wine to ponder over a big purchase, the store also houses a delightful restaurant.

I can’t wait to visit the shop again, but first  … I need to start saving up.

650-652, Chaussée de Louvain, open from Monday until Saturday, between 10.30-19.00

Love homemade cake? Love rare teas and jams from the other side of the world? Me too! And Les Trouvailles de Louise is the place to visit to discover all of it.

Lovely. That’s how I’d describe this tea-room cum little grocery in Schaerbeek. You’ll find it just off the big Avenue Louis Bertrand.

The front of the place is a little shop with spices from exotic countries, crisps, all sorts of tea, jams and chocolate. Little treats in a pretty package. The rest of the shop is a tea-room with charming tables, set with china plates and cups.

But who is Louise? The host and baker of Les Trouvailles. Originally from the Philippines, she came to Brussels a while ago and decided to try her luck opening a tea-room in Schaerbeek.

I’m so glad she did, because Les Trouvailles is such a genuine gem. The banana muffins, the carrot cake, the chocolate brownies are divine. The English breakfast is very tasty, and the sunny garden is a big asset!

290, Rue du Josaphat, closed on Wednesdays, open from 11.00-18.00 on other days (from 09.00 onwards on Saturdays)
