Lucien Cravate

Posted by Bruxelles la Belle on September 12, 2011 in City Centre, Homeware |
Subscribe

While I’m not the biggest fan of vintage clothing, I’m really fond of ‘accessories’ from the past. Orange plastic plates, cute little statues … I could browse Lucien Cravate for hours (but luckily the shop is so small it only takes minutes to scan the shelves).

It’s difficult to walk past the shop without at least an intense peak at the window. There simply are too many bright coloured items on display: homeware items, toys, postcards …

So what usually happens is … I open the door and visit the shop … (what did you think?). I don’t often buy stuff there, but I always leave the shop with a big smile. The retro items are too charming, and some of them are dead funny. Cooking books from the 60s or 70s promising to turn you into the perfect housewife, cute greeting cards (still wrapped), bambi statues, neon smiley badges …

Prices are correct, and for only a few euro you could end up with some of the coolest things for decades ago.

24 Rue des Chartreurx, open from Wednesday until Saturday, 12.00-18.30
