Some of them are ugly, some delicate, some kitsch … If you’ve had enough of that, Le Typographe sells a great alternative: quality paper ones with a simple print, handmade in Brussels.

The shop and ‘factory’ are located in a big mansion in Ixelles. There’s classical music playing in the background, and paper and stationary items on shelves and tables.

During the holidays, the greeting cards are the most popular. Printed with an old Heidelberg letter press, they come in a couple of sizes and colours. If you’re going for something unique, you can make your own by choosing paper, envelopes and draw or write your own design (the choice is overwhelming), or go for a special print and ask the staff at Le Typographe for help.

The shop is perfect for last-minute gift picking as well. From notebooks, to do-lists and calendars (all made in-house) to fancy writing pens and pouches to store your pencils.

And while it might be too late to buy this year’s Christmas cards and have them sent on time, think about Le Typographe for next year (or any upcoming birthday). You can’t go wrong with made in Brussels.

67 Rue Américaine, open from Monday until Friday between 13.00-18.00, Saturday from 11.30-18.00

It’s called Kure and sells a mix of great Scandinavian and French brands, lovely accessories, industrial furniture and art. 

Multi-brand shops are my favourites. There’s a little bit of everything and they’re the perfect place to discover new labels.

Kure opened where the old G-Star shop was, in an old, huge building with two floors, bare brick walls and white ceilings. Amongst the many brands, a couple of great names such as Vanessa Bruno and American Retro. A few labels I had heard of before but never tried myself, such as Hope and Margaux Lonnberg and a couple of names that were completely new to me such as Anine Bing and Ganni.

The style of the clothing is very cool and temporary. Think of white tops and t-shirts, sweaters, black skinny trousers and leather jackets. There are some on-trend pieces as well, such as a checked woollen oversized coat, royal blue fluffy jumpers or flat leopatd boots.

I was very happy to see Kure stocked Fashionology as well, a Dutch jewellery label. Affordable rings, necklaces and bracelets, I had checked out on their website before but never made up my mind. I went home with a small selection of my favourite pieces, and have the feeling my collection will grow soon.

The shop has a little garden too, where you can sit down, read magazines or have a drink.

Note: only women’s clothing at the moment, but there will be stuff for men as well.

I use it to tell time, read e-mails, update Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, call, text … non-stop. So when my dear iphone’s microphone stopped working, I was in trouble.

I went to the shop where I bought it, where I was told it would take minimum 3 weeks. I was entitled to a replacement phone (no smart phone), but that wasn’t good enough an offer. The Apple repair service at Cami would need at least a week, which didn’t sound ideal either. A desperate tweet later, a follower (thanks!!!) informed me of a new smart phone repair shop on Place Chatelain, located in the White Night store.

I was a bit sceptical (why would a repair shop be located in a day/night shop?) but headed over there the next day. You need to walk down the stairs of the shop, where a team of young technicians awaits your broken smartphone. They asked me a couple of questions, promised the problem could easily be solved and that it would be ready in 15 minutes. (Their tag line says ‘ready in an hour’, so I was a bit sceptical (again, yes)).

There’s no possibility to pay with your card (yet), so I walked up to the nearest ATM, and by the time I got back my phone was fixed!

Yes, I did have to pay 60 euro, but I only had to miss my phone for a couple of minutes, which is a fair deal.

They repair most smart phones and tablets, and if they have the spare parts in stock, you’ll have your gadget fixed in the blink of an eye.

Kudos to the young guys who set up this business. They found the gap in the market and the shop in Ixelles is the second one (the other one is in Waterloo), others may follow soon.

43 Place du Chatelain, open from Monday until Friday between 12.00-20.00

If you are in the area, pop in. It will be an amazing discovery of some of Mother Nature’s oldest remains.
