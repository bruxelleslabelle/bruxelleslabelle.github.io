Finally. A post on the Marc by Marc Jacobs store. The shop opening got delayed by more than a year, but the entire Marc by Marc Jacobs collection is now available in Brussels.

There were many rumours as to why it took so long, but let’s forget about those. The shop is open and that’s all that matters. If you’re a fan of the brand, that is.

On the ground floor, you’ll find women’s clothes, shoes, watches, perfumes, bags and accessories. On the top floor, you’ll have the men’s collection, kids collection as well as BookMarc, a selection of books and stationary items.

The friendly sales man told me the top floor will get a bar as well. As of November, you can join ‘Marc’ for a drink. Sounds fun!

And as for the looks of the shop … it’s a complete different story than in Paris. Over there, the shop is dark and has got something of a thrift shop. The one in Brussels is light and spacey, the way all the new MBMJ shop look like these days.

So what do you think? What is worth the wait? I quite like the shop, and think it’s a good addition to the area. After all, Brussels is the capital of Europe, so we should have all the big designer shops, no?

90 Rue Antoine Dansaert, open EVERY DAY between 12.00-20.00

]]> http://www.bruxelleslabelle.com/%3Ffeed=rss2&p=1134 2 Les Enfants d’Edouard http://www.bruxelleslabelle.com/%3Fp=1122 http://www.bruxelleslabelle.com/%3Fp=1122#comments Sun, 17 Jun 2012 20:16:34 +0000 http://www.bruxelleslabelle.com/%3Fp=1122 This could well be the oldest vintage/second-hand shop in Brussels. It has been open for almost 40 years, and was founded by two friends whose father was called Edouard (hence the name). 

The shop is located in a beautiful and huge old mansion. That alone is worth a visit. Once inside, you’ll be overwhelmed by the racks of clothes. Trousers, blazers, skirts, dresses, shirts … there’s an overload of everything. From Chanel to Dries Van Noten, the list of designers and brands is endless too.

And when you think you’ll never be able to browse all the things on the ground floor, know that there’s a big staircase taking you to the second floor with even more clothes and shoes.

Accessories and handbags are displayed in pretty wooden cupboard and on shelves, and there are a couple of fitting rooms and enough mirrors to try stuff on.

A lot of the clothes and shoes come from people who sell them through the shop. If you have some pretty designers stuff in your wardrobe you longer wear or that no longer fits, make an appointment with the shop and let them sell it for you. Modern recycling.

Note: I only visited the shop for women, but there’s one for men too, just next door.

175-177 Avenue Louise, open from Monday until Saturday between 10.00-18.30

The team opened a new shop in Uccle, including a little eatery and space for home decoration, on top of their ever-so-large range of clothes.

The shop opened a couple of weeks ago, but it took me a while to get there as it’s located in an area I rarely visit. But I finally made it!

I loved the entrance door. Might sound silly, but you actually have to pull down a handle to open the big glass door, rather than walking through an open door or waiting for the automatic ones to slide open. First thing you noticed is the eatery, where you can sit down for breakfast, lunch or afternoon cake. They serve salads, sandwiches and cupcakes, typical girl food! One wall of the eatery is dedicated to a selection of tea, jams, coffee syrups (by Monin) in all flavours etc, perfect if you’d like to play fancy tearoom in your own kitchen.

It’s only a few steps from the eatery to the clothes section, where you’ll find some great brands together: Zadig & Voltaire, Bel Air, MiH, McQ by Alexander McQueen, American Vintage, Iro (and more)… and also some shoes by Marc by Marc Jacobs. The style of the items picked is the same as you’ll find in the other No Concept stores: a mix of great basics and more edgy/rock & roll pieces. Perfect!

The last room of the shop is dedicated to your interior. Plates, glasses, mugs, candles and the like.

I was tempted by many things, but left the shop empty-handed (mainly because I’m under a self-imposed shopping ban). On my No Concept Market wish list: the red paprika jam from the eatery and the blue Bel Air jumper with golden elbow pads.

Look for No Concept on Facebook to stay updated on the new collection and the events.
