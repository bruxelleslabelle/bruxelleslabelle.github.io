Färm

Posted by Bruxelles la Belle on November 11, 2013 in City Centre, Eat |
Subscribe

I like to eat healthily and know where my food came from. I buy organic when I can, as local as possible and avoid ready-made food full of preservatives. So when I heard of a new, big organic supermaket, right in the city centre, I planned a visit as soon as I could.

I have an organic shop around the corner, but it’s a very small (and expensive) one. Färm is 380 square metres large and stocks (nearly) everything your normal supermarket would too.

There’s a large refrigerated room for fruit and vegetables, and a fridge isle with milk, pre-packed cheese and meat replacers. In the open counter, you’ll find cheese and a small selection of meat. The choice of bread and pastry is quite large, and the same goes for the racks of crisps and biscuits. Lots of wines, beers, juices, lemonades, oils and vinegars are available as well. And in the non-food section you’ll have cleaning products as well as all sorts of soap, lotions and potions.

The location is quite interesting too. It used to be a hall for public sales, and some of its old features (such as the ‘vente publique’ sign) have been kept in the new shop.

Also interesting, the shop is a co-op. Meaning you can buy your share of Färm (20 euro per share) and decide along with the other share holder what the future of the shop should hold.

Even though Färm is nothing like a ‘real’ farm shop in England for instance, I thoroughly enjoyed wandering about. I chose a loaf of spelt sourdough bread, a piece of marbled cake, dried apple slices, elderflower lemonade, yoghurt and apple juice from a farm just outside Brussels.

Good to know: the owners dream of a very large organic shop with an in-house bakery and butcher’s. That sounds great right? ‘All’ they need is a commercial space of at least 1000 square metres. I do hope they find one soon, and in Brussels of course.

43 Quai au Bois à Brûler, open every day but Sunday between 09.00-20.00
